What it means to be a Groupie

4/26/2014

Enumerators are a great way to search and sort through collection of data within ruby. Today we are going to focus on the group_by Enumerable and its uses.

The group_by syntax looks like this:

.group_by { |object| block} => a_hash

This basically says that what you call on group_by to sort your information you will decide how you want to group your information using the block of code and it will return a hash (a directory of unique keys and their values.)

Why would you need the group_by Enumerable you ask? Well if you are looking to make groups of specific information and narrow down your search this is a huge help.

Everything is better with examples

You can see for the number 1 through 6 it grouping them by their divisibility by 3. 3 and 6 are evenly divisable by 3 with zero remainders, 1 and 4 have one remainder, 2 and 5 have 2 remainders.
